Field Practices

Tank-Specific Safety Protocols

01

Confined Space Entry

All interior tank work is conducted under a written confined space entry permit program. Atmospheric monitoring, attendant/entrant roles, and rescue procedures are established before any entry. Compliant with OSHA 29 CFR 1910.146 and 1926.1201.

02

Fall Protection at Elevation

100% tie-off required for all work above 6 feet. Engineered anchor points, personal fall arrest systems (PFAS), and rescue plans are established for every elevated scope. Crew is trained to OSHA 1926 Subpart M standards.

03

Full Containment Systems

Engineered containment is erected before any blasting or coating begins. Containment is designed to capture 100% of blast media and coating overspray, prevent environmental release, and comply with state and federal air quality regulations.

04

Lead Abatement & Hazardous Material Handling

Lead-containing coatings are identified, tested, and handled per EPA RRP rules and OSHA 1926.62. Workers are medically monitored, equipped with appropriate respiratory protection, and trained in decontamination procedures.

05

SSPC Surface Preparation Standards

All surface preparation is executed to the specified SSPC-SP standard (SP-6 Commercial Blast, SP-10 Near-White, SP-5 White Metal) with anchor profile verification. No coating is applied until surface conditions are confirmed by a certified inspector.

06

Respiratory Protection Program

Written respiratory protection program per OSHA 1910.134. Fit testing, medical evaluations, and proper respirator selection for each task — blasting, coating application, and lead abatement operations each require specific protection levels.
